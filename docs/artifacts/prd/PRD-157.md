## 1. Problem Statement

### What problem are we solving?

AgentX has strong building blocks for planning, execution, review, memory, and harness validation, but the loop from solved work to reusable institutional knowledge is still partial and inconsistent. Relevant learnings are not surfaced in a concrete, ranked way during planning and review, agent-native parity is not audited as a first-class architectural concern, and important review findings can remain transient instead of becoming durable follow-up work.

### Why is this important?

This initiative reduces repeated mistakes, improves review quality, and makes AgentX's agent-centric architecture more deliberate. It also turns isolated problem-solving wins into reusable guidance that improves future sessions across local and GitHub-backed workflows.

### What happens if we do not solve this?

AgentX will continue to lose valuable solved-problem context between sessions, planning will remain weaker than it should be when prior learnings exist, agent-visible capabilities may drift behind user-visible features, and review findings will continue to leak value when they are not converted into trackable follow-up work.

## 4. Research Summary

### Sources Consulted

- Internal AgentX spec and PRD material for memory, review, and workflow behavior
- Internal runtime and extension surfaces for self-review, memory, and harness evaluation
- Internal planning and backlog decomposition notes for this initiative

### Key Findings

1. The strongest requirement is not a larger reviewer roster but an explicit loop of plan -> work -> review -> solution capture -> future reuse.
2. A dedicated learnings-researcher workflow is materially stronger than generic search because it pre-filters by metadata, narrows by category, ranks relevance, and returns distilled guidance.
3. The agent-native reviewer concept is architecturally valuable because it checks action parity, context parity, and shared workspace assumptions instead of generic code style.
4. File-based tracking of review findings is useful only if it integrates with the repo's existing backlog model; a parallel tracking system would add more confusion than value.
5. AgentX already has partial foundations in its continuous learning loop spec, memory system, self-review loop, and harness evaluator, but those pieces are not yet turned into one explicit productized capability.

### Chosen Approach Rationale

Adopt the explicit knowledge-compounding loop, ranked learnings retrieval, and agent-native review lens as AgentX-native capabilities, while adapting durable findings to AgentX's issue/progress model rather than introducing a parallel tracking system.

### Rejected Alternatives

- Expand AgentX with a large copied specialist roster and verbose workflow text.
   Reason: Too coupled to a different operating model and too verbose for AgentX's role-contract model.
- Introduce a separate standalone file-based backlog beside AgentX issues.
  Reason: Would fragment tracking and duplicate workflow state.
- Make agent-native review a hard gate immediately.
  Reason: Advisory-first rollout is lower risk and allows rubric tuning before enforcement.

## 5. Requirements

### 5.1 Functional Requirements

#### Must Have (P0)

1. **Explicit knowledge-compounding workflow contract**
   - **User Story**: As an AgentX maintainer, I want explicit artifact and handoff rules from planning through solution capture so that solved work consistently becomes reusable knowledge.
   - **Acceptance Criteria**:
   - [ ] Workflow phases define required artifacts, handoffs, and completion conditions
   - [ ] Knowledge capture is explicit in the flow rather than implied
   - [ ] Local and GitHub-backed modes are both considered in the contract

2. **Ranked institutional learnings retrieval**
   - **User Story**: As a planner or reviewer, I want relevant prior solutions pre-filtered and ranked so that I can start with distilled guidance instead of rediscovering known patterns.
   - **Acceptance Criteria**:
   - [ ] Learnings retrieval supports metadata pre-filtering and relevance ranking
   - [ ] Returned output is distilled guidance, not raw dumps
   - [ ] Planning and review entry points share the retrieval contract

3. **Agent-native review and parity audit**
   - **User Story**: As a reviewer, I want to check whether user-visible actions are equally available to agents so that agent-centric architectural gaps are caught early.
   - **Acceptance Criteria**:
   - [ ] The review model covers action parity, context parity, and shared workspace assumptions
   - [ ] The output includes a capability map and repeatable scoring rubric
   - [ ] Initial rollout is advisory-first

#### Should Have (P1)

1. **Durable review-finding record integrated with AgentX backlog patterns**
   - **User Story**: As a maintainer, I want approved important findings to become durable tracked work so that deferred issues do not disappear after review.
   - **Acceptance Criteria**:
   - [ ] Findings support status, priority, owner, dependencies, and evidence links
   - [ ] The design reuses AgentX issue/progress patterns
   - [ ] Promotion rules from review finding to backlog item are defined

2. **Workflow-contract discipline without host-specific ceremony**
   - **User Story**: As a product owner, I want workflow contracts to be explicit without coupling AgentX to another tool's slash-command semantics.
   - **Acceptance Criteria**:
   - [ ] Workflow steps are expressed in AgentX-native terms
   - [ ] Tool- or host-specific assumptions are excluded from the product contract

#### Could Have (P2)

1. **Feedback-driven promotion and decay of reusable learnings**
   - **User Story**: As a maintainer, I want learnings to gain or lose confidence over time so that stale or weak guidance does not dominate retrieval.

2. **Expanded reporting surfaces for learnings reuse and parity health**
   - **User Story**: As an operator, I want to see where learnings and agent-native audits are helping so that the initiative can be tuned with evidence.

#### Will Not Have (Out of Scope)

- Replacing the existing memory subsystem wholesale
- Replacing AgentX issues with a new parallel todo repository model
- Immediate hard-gate enforcement of agent-native review findings
- Wholesale copying of external agent or skill definitions

- **Grounding data**: Repo-local plans, reviews, solutions, issue history, and selected runtime state
- **Data sensitivity**: Internal
- **Volume**: Low to medium, scoped to planning/review contexts rather than full-repo dumps

#### Responsible AI Requirements

| Concern | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|

| **Transparency** | Explain why a learning or audit finding was surfaced |
| **Fairness** | Not a primary concern; focus on consistent retrieval and review criteria |
| **Privacy** | Respect current repo-local and platform-local boundaries |
| **Human Oversight** | Advisory-first for agent-native review and durable finding promotion |
| **Accountability** | Preserve evidence links and backlog traceability |

#### AI-Specific Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Retrieval first narrows deterministically before asking models to synthesize
- [ ] Learnings and review outputs include enough rationale for human validation
- [ ] Fallback behavior exists when no relevant prior learning is found
- [ ] Advisory-first outputs can later be promoted into stronger gates without redesign

### 5.3 Non-Functional Requirements

#### Performance
- Learnings retrieval should favor metadata narrowing before expensive synthesis
- Review augmentation should not materially degrade normal planning/review usability

#### Security
- Reuse existing repository and tool boundary rules
- Do not expose hidden user-only capabilities through agent review outputs without explicit design approval

#### Scalability
- The design should scale across local mode and GitHub mode
- The learnings model should support more artifacts over time without requiring full-content scans for every request

#### Reliability
- Advisory outputs must be understandable even when the system finds no relevant learnings
- Durable findings promotion must not create duplicate or conflicting tracking records by default

## 7. User Flows

### Flow 1: Planning with Prior Learnings

1. User or agent starts a planning workflow.
2. AgentX can start with an explicit brainstorm surface that frames the active issue, relevant constraints, and the top ranked planning learnings.
3. AgentX checks for relevant institutional learnings using metadata narrowing and ranking.
4. AgentX returns distilled learnings and integrates them into planning context.
5. Planning proceeds with explicit references to known pitfalls, patterns, and reused decisions.

### Flow 2: Solved Work Becomes Reusable Knowledge

1. A task is completed and validated.
2. AgentX determines whether post-solution capture is required or recommended.
3. Operators and agents can inspect an explicit compound-loop surface that combines capture readiness, relevant learnings, and promotable review findings.
4. A reusable learning artifact is created or updated with enough metadata for later retrieval, preferably from an active-context scaffold instead of a blank file.
5. Future planning and review flows can surface the learning when relevant.

### Flow 3: Agent-Native Review

1. A feature or change reaches review.
2. AgentX evaluates whether user-visible actions and context are available to agents.
3. Advisory findings identify parity gaps and classify their severity.
4. Important gaps either block future promotion or become tracked follow-up work.

### Flow 4: Durable Review Follow-up

1. Reviewer identifies an important but deferred finding.
2. Finding is converted into a durable review-finding record.
3. Record is triaged into tracked backlog work when appropriate.
4. The lifecycle from pending to ready to complete stays visible in AgentX's existing workflow model.

## 8. Dependencies and Constraints

### Dependencies

- Existing AgentX memory and lesson concepts in internal specs
- Existing self-review and reviewer workflow surfaces
- Existing issue and progress tracking model across local and GitHub-backed workflows
- Existing documentation directories for PRDs, reviews, plans, and solution-like artifacts

### Constraints

- Must preserve AgentX-native workflow language and avoid host-specific ceremony
- Must fit the current issue/progress model rather than create a parallel backlog system
- Must remain advisory-first for agent-native review until confidence is proven
- Must keep deterministic narrowing central to learnings retrieval to control noise and cost

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Learnings retrieval becomes noisy | Users ignore results | Use deterministic narrowing first and keep evidence/rationale visible |
| Workflow contract becomes too verbose or tool-specific | Adoption friction | Keep the contract focused on artifacts and handoffs, not host syntax |
| Agent-native review overreaches into subjective style | Low trust | Keep the rubric architectural and capability-focused |
| Durable findings create duplicate tracking records | Backlog clutter | Define promotion rules and reuse existing issue/progress patterns |
| Solution capture becomes burdensome | Low capture rates | Make required vs optional capture scenarios explicit |
